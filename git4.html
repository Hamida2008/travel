Located near the foot of the South Pier of the Sydney Harbour Bridge and facing directly towards the Opera House, it is quite literally in the heart of Sydney’s glamorous, vibrant Circular Quay and Rocks district. A true five-star luxury hotel, it has recently undergone its largest and most comprehensive transformation in its 21-year history.

Nestled atop rust-red dunes, Longitude 131° overlooks Australia’s best-known natural icon, Uluru, and is arguably one of the best luxury wilderness camps in the world. The welcome open spaces of the central Dune House include the restaurant with ever-changing views of the mysterious monolith Uluru as a backdrop for every meal, as well as lounging areas in contemporary campaign style, ideal for relaxing with an icy drink and exchanging stories of the day’s outback adventures.

Set on the northernmost tip of the Great Barrier Reef, Lizard Island is a leading luxury lodge experience in one of the world’s most renowned natural environments. Nestled within a sprawling national park enriched with diverse Australian fauna and flora, with direct access to the world’s largest coral reef system and home to only 40 exclusive rooms and suites, including the acclaimed House at Lizard Island, this island retreat promises an immersive escape.
